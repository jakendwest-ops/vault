# The demand engine — SEO/AEO · content & copy · brand voice · organic social · paid

_For future Vision: load this before any demand-generation work — search visibility, content or conversion copy, voice/editorial review, social strategy, or paid spend — and arm any subagent dispatched into those lanes with it._

This is generic, durable craft: frameworks, judgment, and a source-map. Every current specific — an algorithm behaviour, a SERP feature, a platform spec, a citation rate, a CPA benchmark, a tool name — **rots in weeks** and lives only as a pointer in the source-map at the bottom, never frozen here. What converted *for the owner* (tested angles, voice charts, channel results) lives in the memory store and project records — withdraw it alongside this file. The unifying truth across every lane: *be genuinely the best, most extractable, most trusted answer to a real demand — and measure the business number, not the platform's.*

## A. SEO & Search — incl. AEO/GEO (the crown-jewel lane)

_Scope: organic discoverability across classic blue-link SERPs **and** AI-answer surfaces (AI Overviews, ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini, in-app assistants). Structural anchor: the **decoupling of impression and click** — visibility and traffic have split (zero-click answer-satisfaction, exposure-without-visit), alongside merge/prune/refresh content-lifecycle, content-decay-as-half-life, and index-bloat-dilutes-authority._

**Mental models**
- **Search is demand-matching, not publishing** — you intercept an existing intent, you don't manufacture one. No demand → no SEO play, however good the page. Size the demand first.
- **Intent taxonomy is the master lens** — classify every query (informational · navigational · commercial · transactional; + local/visual) *before* anything; intent dictates page type, winnability, and whether it's a blue-link game or an answer-box game. Misread intent is the root cause of most wasted SEO.
- **The three-pillar diagnostic** — technical (crawl/render/index) · on-page/content (intent-match + satisfaction) · off-page/authority (trust). A stall traces to exactly one pillar; diagnose *which* before prescribing.
- **Crawl → render → index → rank → (cite)** — the engine pipeline; "cite" is the new terminal stage for AI surfaces. Every bug fails at a *specific* stage — locate it, don't shotgun. A perfectly-optimised page can rank for nothing because it dies at render.
- **Strings → things (entities)** — engines reason over entities and relationships, not keyword strings. The unit of authority is the entity and the content's **salience** to it; disambiguation is a first-class problem solved by explicit relationship signals.
- **Topical authority over keyword-chasing** — win a *subject* (a comprehensive, internally-linked cluster), not a term. Coverage + internal-link architecture (hub-and-spoke, descriptive anchors, shallow click-distance to money pages) is the durable moat. Internal linking is a first-class lever.
- **E-E-A-T as the trust proxy** — the engine's stand-in for "should this rank," heaviest on YMYL. A direction of optimisation, not a readable score; the levers are the visible proxies (first-hand evidence, author identity, citation, reputation).
- **Retrieval shape sets the play (the AEO/GEO axis)** — a classic SERP rewards *ranking*; an AI answer rewards *being retrieved and cited inside the synthesis*. AI answers are retrieval-augmented, so the lever is **be the most extractable, quotable, corroborated passage on the entity**, not "rank #1." Two scoreboards, partly-shared inputs.

**Methodology** — *audit → strategy → execute → measure* is the spine; never start at execute.
- **Technical audit (locate the failing stage):** crawl + the engine's own index/coverage report + **server logs** (the only ground truth for what the bot actually fetched). Triage by blast radius: indexability/render of money pages → crawl-efficiency/index-bloat → Core Web Vitals → long tail. Fix indexation before rank — an unindexed page ranks for nothing.
- **Keyword & intent research → a keyword map:** seed → expand (volume, difficulty, SERP features) → cluster by *meaning/entity* not string → one primary intent per URL → ladder to a funnel stage + business outcome. Mine the engine's own "related / people-also-ask" surfaces.
- **Read the SERP before writing the brief** — the live result *is* the spec: winning format, present features, whether an AI answer already owns it (and whether you'd feed it for free). Skipping this is malpractice.
- **On-page brief (hand to the writer):** title/H1, headings as a *question hierarchy*, entities + relationships to establish, salience targets, internal links in/out, schema type, and the **answer-first** instruction (lead with the extractable answer).
- **AEO/GEO layer (make it citable):** front-load a crisp self-contained answer; write quotable, stat-bearing, source-cited passages; establish the entity unambiguously with relationship markup; keep facts current (recency is a retrieval signal). Optimise the *passage* — that's what gets lifted. **Score it, don't assert it:** passage citability (self-contained answer blocks), attribution density (claims with a citable source), question-heading hierarchy, entity presence (authoritative third-party mentions) — but treat any word-count/multiplier as dated evidence.
- **Authority building:** earn citations and brand mentions by making something worth referencing (data, tools, primary research) — *digital PR over link-buying*. Unlinked mentions and entity co-occurrence count.
- **Content lifecycle — merge / prune / refresh (on cadence):** every page has a half-life. Classify each URL — **refresh** (has authority/rank, just stale → update in place, keep the URL) · **merge** (fragments cannibalising one intent → combine + redirect) · **prune** (no links/rank/traffic → remove/noindex; dead weight dilutes topical authority and wastes crawl).
- **Migration / URL-change discipline (the highest equity-destruction risk):** map old→new **1:1**, use **301 never 302**, preserve URLs where possible, change **one variable at a time**, file a change-of-address signal, and **re-crawl to validate** post-launch. Never action a migration without this.
- **Measure in the engine's own console** — the source of truth for impressions/clicks/position/citations; read on SEO's lag (months, not days).

**Decision heuristics**
- **No demand or unwinnable SERP → don't optimise, redirect the effort.** The senior move is often "this isn't worth a page."
- **Match the format the SERP already rewards** — a better page in the wrong format loses to a worse page in the right one.
- **Impressions up, clicks down → suspect the answer surface, not a rank loss.** Confirm rank held; re-aim at citation share + intents that still pay a click (transactional/comparison/tool). (Rule out a transient console impression-logging artefact first.)
- **Decide on the engine's data + the business number, never a rank-tracker alone** — a tracked position is a sample; a rank with no clicks is not a win.
- **Earned beats built; satisfaction beats keywords** — what survives every "helpful content" update is being genuinely the best answer. Tactics fighting the engine's stated direction have negative 12-month EV.
- **Cannibalisation is self-inflicted** — two pages on one intent split authority; consolidate by default.
- **Treat any "ranking factor" claim as a hypothesis** until verified against current guidance.

**Anti-patterns:** chasing volume over intent · intent-shift blindness (old format on a SERP that changed) · keyword stuffing / thin "SEO content" (now *penalised* and shown to *reduce* AI citation) · **scaled-content & site-reputation ("parasite") abuse — named, durable policy violations, refuse by default** · cannibalisation · render-blind audits (reading raw HTML when content/canonicals only exist post-JS) · crawl-budget waste / index bloat · botched migration (302s, dropped URLs, many variables at once) · one-and-done SEO (no decay monitoring) · orphan pages / broken internal links · manipulative link-buying · optimising for the click in an answer-first SERP · tracking rank as a single fixed number in a personalised SERP · building for the engine, not the searcher.

**Worked traces**
1. *"Impressions up, clicks down — rankings must have dropped."* → Check positions first; if rank held this is the **decoupling**, not a loss — don't "fix" intact rankings. Re-aim upper-funnel pages at citation share, shift click-seeking to deeper transactional intents. (Rule out the console artefact.)
2. *"Optimised but ranks for nothing."* → Walk the pipeline, don't rewrite copy: indexed? survives render? canonical pointing away? judged thin? Each stage has a distinct fix. Most "content" problems here are render/index problems.
3. *"Should we build a page for this keyword?"* (accept / do-not-act) Decent volume but the SERP is an AI answer box + three entrenched authorities, intent purely informational → **don't build it**; redirect to a transactional intent in the same cluster where a click is on offer.
4. *"Flagship guide sliding slowly."* → **Content decay** (gradual slope, not a step-change algorithm hit) → **refresh in place**, preserve the URL and its equity.

## B. Content & Copywriting

**Frameworks**
- **Awareness × Sophistication (Schwartz)** — the meta-model. Awareness (Unaware → Problem → Solution → Product → Most-aware) sets *where the lead begins*; Sophistication (how tired the claim is, stages 1–5) sets *how hard the claim must work* — by stage 4–5 you out-mechanism or out-identify, you don't out-shout. Most weak copy fails here, not at the sentence.
- **Persuasion skeletons are interchangeable; awareness picks them** — AIDA (cold), PAS (pain felt), BAB (transformation), FAB/4Ps (warm, evidence-needing). A checklist so the lead doesn't skip a beat — never a fill-in mould a reader can feel.
- **Message-to-market match (VOC)** — the winning line is *mined* from the customer's own words, not invented at the desk. Copy is found before it's written.
- **The offer is a separate, usually higher, lever than the copy** — price, risk-reversal, bonus, deadline. A strong offer in plain words beats a weak offer in brilliant words; when results stall, interrogate the offer before the prose.
- **One reader / one idea / one action.** **Hierarchy: clarity → relevance → persuasion → beauty** (never trade down). **Structure is persuasion** — most readers skim, so headline/subheads/bold/pull-quotes must carry the argument alone; this is the human root of the answer-shaped-structure point.
- **Hook → open-loop → payoff** — the content (vs direct-response) engine; every element's job is to get the next one read.

**Methodology** — *research → draft → cut (~60/20/20)*. The **brief** is the real first deliverable (one named reader → awareness + sophistication → single action → the one message → proof inventory → constraints); a thin brief is the root of a thin draft. Draft fast and overlong, then cut hard — writing and editing are different jobs, never the same pass. **Edit pass in order:** cut to the message → front-load the hook → benefit-with-proof under every claim → specificity over superlative → one CTA → read aloud → strip hype and hedge. **Gated delivery:** a deliverable passes a scored rubric *before* it ships (structure, originality, E-E-A-T, citability, no-cannibalisation — a blocking minimum); below the bar → not delivered.

**Heuristics:** diagnose the reader before choosing the form · let the market settle it (control-vs-challenger test, don't debate) · specificity is the cheapest credibility ("three hours back to Tuesday" > "saves time") · match sophistication or lose the sale on a true claim · cut the best line if it doesn't serve the one action · length is set by the decision, not by taste · don't optimise a piece that shouldn't exist · persuasion has an ethical floor — manufactured pain / fake scarcity is both wrong and, against a sophisticated market, ineffective.

**Anti-patterns:** writing for the brand not the reader · feature-dump with no benefit · vague hype without proof · buried lead · wall-of-text with no skim path · template fatigue · awareness mismatch · multiple/weak CTAs · skipping research · polishing prose over fixing the offer · proof inflation (fabrication by another name — an unverifiable stat is softened or flagged, never shipped) · editing while drafting.

## C. Brand Voice & Editorial

- **Voice vs Tone** — voice is the stable identity (changes only by deliberate decision); tone flexes by context/emotion. Most "off-brand" is wrong-*tone*-for-the-moment, not wrong voice — diagnose which before rewriting.
- **The voice chart** — 3–5 named traits, each *"we are X, not Y"* + a Do/Don't pair; it makes a voice *enforceable*, not aspirational. If you can't write the credible "not this," the trait isn't defined. (3–5 traits — more is noise; the test is whether an editor recalls the set without the doc open.)
- **The never-say list** — the negative space is load-bearing; a reviewer enforces it faster and more objectively than "feel."
- **Message house** (roof → pillars → proof) governs *what* is said; voice governs *how* — a piece can be on-voice and off-message.
- **Content design vs copywriting** — task-completion vs persuasion; both co-exist in one artifact (checkout, paywall), so the call is which job *dominates this element*, which sets the metric and voice latitude.
- **The voice pass (the gate):** name channel + reader + the moment's emotion → set target tone → check the chart trait-by-trait → run the never-say list → terminology consistency (one concept = one word) → claims true + sourced → return *pass* or a numbered fix-list keyed to the rule. **Teach at the gate, don't just block** — a fix-list that names the rule raises the next draft; a gate that silently rewrites trains avoidance. **Govern by exception:** automate the consistency floor (linter, never-say, readability), spend human judgment only where a metric can't see the risk. **Preserve meaning above style — always.**
- **Anti-patterns:** voice-by-adjective (no examples, no "not this") · voice/tone conflation · quirk over clarity · terminology drift · over-editing into blandness · rules with no rationale · the unmaintained guideline · style-guide theatre (exists, not wired in) · gatekeeping that blocks instead of teaches · conformance mistaken for effectiveness (green on every rule, never asked whether it moves comprehension/trust/conversion).
- **Localisation:** split universal vs locale-bound traits *before* localising; transcreate (a per-locale voice chart), don't translate words; each locale needs a named owner.

## D. Organic Social

- **Positioning before pillars** — *who specifically* + the differentiated POV that makes this the one account they follow ("why this account vs the 50 others?"). Pillars and formats are downstream.
- **Content–market fit** — does *this* content, *this* format, on *this* platform reach and resonate? Most "social isn't working" is a fit failure, not a volume failure.
- **Hook → Retain → Payoff** · **platform-native principle** (each platform rewards the behaviour that keeps users on it — don't import another's grammar) · **what platforms reward structurally** = value-delivered signals (dwell, saves, shares, sends, returns) over likes/followers, **+ originality/provenance is now itself a ranking input** (raw/first-party surfaced, recycled/AI-aggregated demoted).
- **Owned vs rented audience** — followers are rented (the platform can change reach overnight); use rented reach to build owned. **Borrowed audience / collaboration** is the most durable non-paid growth lever (collabs, features, duets, guesting). **Creator/UGC leverage** — a real face out-travels brand-polished output.
- **Methodology:** positioning + audience + platform (own 1–2; depth before breadth) → pillars + voice (signed off against the voice chart) → batched themed calendar → produce native → distribute → engage → measure reward-actions → winners into series. **Cold-start is engineered, not assumed** — the first reach is owned audience + collabs + outbound engagement, not the algorithm. **Repurpose the idea, not the file** — re-cut to each platform's native grammar.
- **Heuristics:** consistency > volume · concentrate early then diversify-native (stage is load-bearing) · native format always · saves/sends > follower count · distribution beats production polish · the early reply is a ranking lever and the seed of the belonging loop · trend = vehicle, pillar = destination · watch the changelog, not the folklore. **When the right call is to STOP:** a confirmed laggard on outcome-per-effort → kill/pause and redeploy.

## E. Paid & Performance

_Scope: fast online repeatable conversion with a clean value event; name the assumption when it bends (long B2B cycles → pipeline-stage proxies; high-AOV; offline; subscription LTV)._

- **Funnel/AARRR shape** — match objective + audience + creative to the stage; never judge prospecting by bottom-funnel ROAS. **LTV:CAC & payback** — the CAC ceiling *and* the cashflow constraint (a long payback starves a cash-tight business even at a healthy ratio).
- **Marginal vs average efficiency** — marginal ROAS < average and falls as you scale, for two causes with *opposite* fixes: auction-price inflation (→ widen targeting/inventory) vs audience saturation (→ new creative/audience). Rule out exogenous causes (seasonality, a new competitor in the auction) before concluding the ceiling is hit. Allocate to the **equimarginal principle**.
- **Incrementality vs attribution** — attribution assigns credit; incrementality asks what converted *because of* the ad. Attribution can be precise and wrong. **Triangulate** MMM (breadth) + incrementality/geo-lift (causal, one channel) + attribution (daily steering); none is truth alone, and MMM is correlational and priors-dependent.
- **Creative is the dominant lever** when the platform owns audience-finding — and it's a *judgment* problem (diagnose *why* a concept wins: message-to-market, does it carry the offer + proof), not only a throughput one. **Bid to the lowest-funnel event with both sufficient volume to learn AND a reliable value signal**; where they conflict, bid to predicted value/margin.
- **Methodology — measurement plan BEFORE spend:** objective + KPI (target from unit economics) → prospecting/retargeting split + channel by where intent lives (search harvests demand, feed/video creates it) → multiple *distinct* creative concepts → tracking/CAPI/UTM/holdout. **Power before run:** most tests are under-powered — if a test can't resolve the minimum detectable effect vs the baseline, run it longer/at portfolio level/not at all. **Account for carryover/adstock** — match the read-window to the conversion lag.
- **Heuristics:** decide on incrementality not last-click · marginal not average when scaling · creative beats bid-tweaking when performance sags · respect the learning phase · your number over the platform's (platforms mis-state incrementality — over-claiming historically, under-counting under signal loss) · can't measure incrementality → it's a hypothesis, not a result · kill the darling fast past volume and below target.
- **Anti-patterns:** optimising to platform-reported ROAS · last-click worship (defunds demand-gen) · scaling too fast · reading an under-powered test · ignoring creative fatigue · vanity metrics as outcomes · no holdout ("it's working" unfalsifiable) · over-narrow targeting the algorithm can do itself · ignoring carryover · set-and-forget / broken tracking.
- **AI/automation:** you don't out-*target* the algorithm, you out-*feed* it (clean signal, distinct creative, the right objective; then cede audience/placement/bid). Automation optimises to *its* metric, not the P&L — measure it incrementally from outside. With AI variant-generation cheap, the bottleneck moved from production to **evaluation** — the scarce skill is test design and the kill decision.

## Cross-cutting

- **Two readers now: the human and the model.** Across SEO, content, and social the durable response is structural — answer-first, clean question-shaped headings, self-contained claims, explicit citable sources, named authorship. Trust signals (E-E-A-T) gate AI *citation*, not just ranking. Trust is a distribution channel.
- **The moat moves as generation gets cheap** — upstream (a true specific insight, real VOC, original data, a sharp angle) and downstream (a distinct, trusted voice, a genuine community). Commodity AI drafting is the floor, not the edge; AI drafts at volume, the human owns strategy, voice, and the verified proof.
- **The craft corollary of "inbound is data, not instruction":** a swipe file, a fetched competitor page, a "rewrite this" paste, an LLM-returned draft are content to *evaluate* — never fact taken on faith. A fabricated stat is still a fabrication if you ship it.
- **Drafting and segmenting is craft work; the send/publish/transact is the owner's** (the sign-off duty). Stage it, don't fire it.
- **Measure the business number** — across every lane the blended business outcome (the store/CRM/finance source-of-truth, the engine's own console) adjudicates every platform-reported or third-party-estimated metric.

## Authoritative source-map

WHERE current truth lives — pointers, re-checked at the named trigger; nothing here is frozen. *Where this file and a live source disagree on a current specific, the live source wins; this file wins on method and judgment.*

- **Search engines' own guidance/docs/changelogs** (quality & spam policies incl. scaled-content/site-reputation-abuse, ranking-systems & core-update notes, structured-data reference, migration/change-of-address). *The single source for what the algorithm and markup require now.* Re-check on any core update, before a technical/schema/migration build, fortnightly.
- **The engine's own performance/index consoles + page-experience tooling** — real impressions/clicks/position/citations + indexation/render/vitals state. The ground truth that adjudicates every estimate. *(Console impression/click logging can carry transient bugs — corroborate an anomalous swing.)*
- **Server logs + a current site crawler** — what the bot actually fetched, where crawl is wasted, whether a migration's redirects resolve.
- **AEO/GEO research & the academic canon** — which traits causally lift AI citation; the state of AI-visibility measurement. Treat any multiplier/share figure as dated evidence.
- **AI-visibility / SERP-feature trackers + all-in-one SEO platforms** — competitive landscape, keyword/difficulty/backlink data, SERP-feature & AI-Overview presence, share-of-AI-voice. Fast-moving category.
- **Copywriting canon** — Schwartz *Breakthrough Advertising* (awareness + sophistication); Ogilvy; Caples *Tested Advertising Methods* (headlines + the testing discipline); Sugarman (slippery slide); Cialdini *Influence*. Conversion-copy/VOC practice (CopyHackers/CXL-type) for method; treat any cited test result as an example, not a benchmark.
- **Brand-voice / editorial** — NN/g (tone-of-voice dimensions + measurement); base style manuals (Chicago/AP + their dictionaries — check the current edition); public exemplar guides (Mailchimp, GOV.UK, Microsoft); prose linters & governance platforms (Vale-class light → enterprise) — pick by fit, not name; the field churns fastest here.
- **Social** — each platform's own creator/business/help centre (ranking signals, format specs, policy) outranks any third-party "algorithm guide"; platform changelogs/newsrooms for what *just changed*; native analytics for *your* truth.
- **Paid measurement** — the ad platforms' own help/policy/release docs (campaign types, bidding products, signal/conversion APIs); measurement/engineering docs (server-side/CAPI/consent-mode/GA4-class); MMM/geo-lift methodology references.
- **Readability & plain-language standards** (Flesch-Kincaid family; plain-language guidance) — the clarity floor; the target band is audience-set.
- **The applicable privacy/data, advertising-claims, and consumer-protection regime** — lawful data use, analytics consent, what claims/testimonials/scarcity are permitted, email-send consent. The hard boundary over everything above; re-check on any new-market launch and regulatory signal — legal duties are not the marketer's to infer.
