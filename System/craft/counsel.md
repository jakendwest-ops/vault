# Counsel craft — strategy, finance, legal & risk, sales, forecasting

_For future Vision: the senior advisory craft — load this (or arm a subagent with it) whenever a task turns on **whether** or **how** a move should be made: strategy and planning, finance and unit economics, legal/risk/compliance, sales and pipeline, forecasting. Durable method lives here; volatile facts — rates, vendors, benchmarks, the live SOTA — live behind the source-map and are pulled fresh, never frozen._

The disciplines share one spine: **diagnose before you prescribe, separate the analysis from the decision, and let stakes × reversibility set the rigour.** Each section adds the senior structure on top of the assumed fundamentals.

---

## Strategy & planning

**Lenses (which one, when):** Rumelt's kernel — *diagnosis → guiding policy → coherent action*; if there's no diagnosis of the crux, it's a wish-list, not a strategy (reach for it first, always). Playing-to-Win cascade (aspiration → where-to-play → how-to-win → capabilities → systems), where WTP and HTW are tested *as a consistent pair*, never listed then bolted together. Helmer's 7 Powers for durability — a power is *superior, significant, and survives rational imitation* (a barrier the rival won't cross, not just hasn't). Porter (Five Forces, generic stances) for "is this even a good game." Wardley mapping for situational awareness when the landscape shifts. The four levels of residual uncertainty (clear future · alternate futures · a range · true ambiguity) is the meta-lens that *selects* the others. JTBD/Value-Proposition Canvas for need-first framing; Blue Ocean/ERRC to refuse a head-to-head game.

**Method (strategy from a mess):** frame the crux (symptom vs cause) → assess the uncertainty level → read the situation (forces, capabilities, unit economics; mark evidence vs assumption) → screen organisational feasibility (who must say yes, whose power it threatens, what the incentive system rewards) → generate 2–3 *genuinely exclusive* options → choose with explicit trade-offs + named kill-criteria → sequence so each move funds the next → make it measurable and pre-mortem before commit. Prioritise on value × confidence × reversibility (not value alone): cheap-reversible-high-learning moves first (they buy information), gate the expensive-irreversible behind a passed test. Translate the chosen bet into ≤3 objectives, each with one *leading* indicator, an owner, and a review cadence against data.

**Where senior diverges from junior:** No sacrifice named → no strategy yet (state what you won't do and who you'll disappoint). Reversibility is the master variable — one-way door, slow down; two-way door, decide fast (treating a reversible call as irreversible is the most common paralysis). Durability beats position ("we're winning now" is not strategy). Clear the value bar, not just coherence — tie the bet to return over a defensible horizon vs the next use of the capital. Separate the strategy verdict from the risk decision. Model the competitor's *next* move, not just this one — a plan that only works if the rival stands still is not a plan. A strategy must survive the org and its incentives, not just the analysis.

**Failure modes:** goals masquerading as strategy ("grow 20%" with no how) · weak/absent diagnosis · strategy-as-a-list (no coherence, edge dies) · product-first not need-first · static-document syndrome (filed, never revisited against data) · comparative-static blindness (planning as if the competitor freezes) · false precision on a genuinely uncertain future · analysis-paralysis ↔ HiPPO gut-call (both skip the option-and-trade-off step) · imitating the winner's position not its power.

## Finance & unit economics

**Lenses:** the three statements as one linked system (net income → retained earnings and → top of cash-flow; ending cash ties BS to CFS; a model that won't balance is telling you an assumption is wrong — never read one statement alone). Accrual ≠ cash — accrual is economic reality, cash is survival, the gap *is* working capital; a profitable firm dies of a cash gap. Value = PV of future cash to the claimant, discounted at its risk (match numerator to denominator: FCFF→WACC→enterprise, FCFE→cost-of-equity→equity). ROIC vs WACC is the value equation — growth below the hurdle destroys value; judge capital allocation on *incremental* ROIC, never the blended average. DuPont to locate why a return moved. Unit economics: contribution margin (price − *all* variable cost), LTV built on contribution margin and *discounted* for time and churn, then CAC payback and LTV:CAC — blended metrics hide cohort decay. Materiality & conservatism govern where to spend effort and which way to round. COSO for the controls model (segregation of duties + reconciliation are load-bearing).

**Method:** Build any model — define the decision it serves → pick the lens (accrual=performance, cash=liquidity, DCF=value) → separate inputs / calculations / outputs, one input entered once, no buried hard-codes → a check row that must equal zero → sensitivities, never a single point estimate. DCF — unlevered FCF over an explicit horizon → discount at WACC → terminal value by Gordon-growth *and* exit-multiple cross-checked → bridge enterprise→equity → present a range (football field); constrain g below WACC and long-run nominal GDP, and consistent with the firm's own reinvestment; TV often runs 60–80% of EV, so stress it, don't bury it. Relative valuation — genuinely comparable set, right multiple to right metric, median with outliers interrogated; a multiple is an opinion about growth and risk. Cost of capital on market-value weights; the discount rate is the most error-prone, most leveraged input — build it, don't pluck it. 13-week direct cash-flow when cash is tight; model the cash-conversion cycle (DSO + DIO − DPO) explicitly, never as a plug. FP&A loop: driver-based plan → rolling forecast → budget-vs-actual → decompose variance into price/volume/mix/rate and narrate the driver. Sensitivity (flex one variable) ≠ scenario (coherent multi-driver states). Earnings-quality read: reconcile reported profit to cash over time; revenue growing ahead of receivables/cash is the red flag (M-score is a screen, not a verdict).

**Where senior diverges from junior:** Cash before profit ("will it make payroll" outranks "what's the margin"). Be roughly right, not precisely wrong — output precision never exceeds input quality. The assumptions are the model — scrutinise the 2–3 drivers that move the answer. Separate the calculation from the business call. Incremental, not average. Rank on NPV, not IRR. Reconciliation is truth — if it doesn't tie to the trial balance/bank/subledger it's wrong until proven otherwise. Conservative assumptions age better. Know the boundary — incorporation choice, complex CGT/VAT selection, audit opinions, regulated tax advice → name the qualified-accountant handoff; the arithmetic is in scope, the regulated opinion is not.

**Failure modes:** profit/cash conflation · hard-codes and broken links, unmanaged circularity, a plug hiding the imbalance · no check row / no version control · false precision (six sig-figs on a guess) · blended metrics hiding decay · aggressive/early revenue recognition · ignoring working capital and timing · survivorship bias in comps · optimisation-to-target (reverse-engineering to hit a valuation) · quoting a rate without its date/jurisdiction · controls as paperwork (a reconciliation signed but not performed).

## Legal, risk & compliance

**Lenses:** Classify-before-advise is the master move — almost every avoidable error is a mis-scope; pin *regulated vs not* (perimeter), *B2C vs B2B*, and *special-category vs ordinary data* first, because each flips the downstream ruleset. Identify the client before advising (in-house: the entity, not the director — privilege runs to the company). IRAC/CRAC as the spine of any read — rule as universal principle, applied element-by-element. Risk = likelihood × impact, but the heat map is a *communication* tool — for material exposure move toward loss-as-a-distribution (FAIR). Inherent → Control → Residual (you carry the residual; report residual to the decision-maker). The 4 T's (Tolerate · Treat · Transfer · Terminate) — pair every risk with one, an owner, and a date; transfer moves money not the duty, and can itself fail (an indemnity is only as good as the indemnitor). COSO ERM (govern/report) vs ISO 31000 (run the process); Three Lines (who must not mark their own homework); Policy → Standard → Procedure → Record; NIST AI RMF (Govern · Map · Measure · Manage) for AI-governance work.

**Method:** Contract review against a *playbook* (preferred / ranked fallbacks / walk-away per clause, set before the deal); go clause-by-clause, side-aware, score routine/negotiate/dealbreaker; output a redline *plus* a plain-language risk summary; caps, indemnities, IP and data first — that's where the money and the duty sit. Risk assessment: scope → identify → analyse (quantify if material) → evaluate vs *appetite* → treat → owner + review date. Regulatory mapping: obligation → rule → control → evidence → owner (the control bridges "the law says" to "we can prove it"). Write the standard to the *outcome* the regulator tests, not the activity. AI-system pass: classify the use-case risk tier → identify the role held (provider duties are heavier than deployer) → map overlapping regimes and reuse one assessment → human oversight + transparency + logging as standing controls.

**Where senior diverges from junior:** Escalate on materiality × novelty × contention, not on topic — routine-and-settled, advise and proceed; material/novel/contentious, a qualified lawyer signs off (naming the line protects everyone). Is it still live? — time-bar before substance. Separate the legal verdict from the risk decision; never accept a third party's risk on their behalf — the authority owns the accept. Outcome beats process, but process is the evidence. Proportionality is a competence — over-papering a low-risk NDA burns the same credibility as under-papering a material MSA; a control costing more than the loss it prevents gets bypassed. Reversibility and blast-radius gate the "stop" — block the irreversible/outward (sign, send money, publish, waive a right, breach a retention duty), advise on the reversible. "It's standard" is not a read (standard *for whom, in which position*). When the law is genuinely uncertain, say so — fabricated certainty is the cardinal sin. Default to the regulator's own words on anything load-bearing; cite the source inline.

**Failure modes:** tick-box / theatre compliance (the modern regime judges the outcome) · reviewing without a playbook · mis-scoping the perimeter (the most expensive error — discards the correct ruleset wholesale) · privilege self-sabotage (mixing legal advice with business commentary in one document) · uncapped liability / one-way indemnity slipping through under deal pressure · risk-register theatre (logged once, never re-scored) · practising law you're not qualified for · confusing "we have a policy" with "we have a control" · no contemporaneous file note (the note written *when* you advised is the defensive artifact) · fixing the clause, not the template · stale-rule reliance (a superseded rule is a wrong rule, not an old one).

## Sales & CRM

**Lenses:** the buyer's job-to-be-done, not the seller's funnel — the deal advances through the *buyer's* non-linear decision process (problem ID → exploration → requirements → selection → validation → consensus); a stage with no buyer-side counterpart is internal fiction. Qualification as a portfolio of risk, not a checklist — MEDDIC/MEDDPICC names where the deal will *die* (no metric → no business case; no economic-buyer access → no budget owner; no proven champion → no internal seller; unmapped paper process → a silent procurement slip). The methodology stack assigned by stage: discovery → SPIN/Gap Selling; qualification → MEDDIC; differentiation → Challenger; close-against-indecision → JOLT; account → MAP + buying-committee map (never run one frame end-to-end). The buying-committee/RACI map makes multithreading deliberate. Compelling event — the dated, externally-anchored consequence of inaction (no compelling event → the timeline is a hope). Mutual action plan built *backwards* from the compelling event — the operational spine of the back half, exposes a slip weeks early. Pipeline velocity = (qualified opps × deal value × win rate) ÷ cycle days — diagnose *which* term is broken first. Revenue ≠ new logos — expansion/renewal (NRR) is the cheaper, higher-probability half.

**Method:** Qualify hybrid (fast first-pass to qualify *out* ruthlessly → MEDDIC for survivors); end at pursue/nurture/disqualify. Discovery: situation → measurable problem → implication/cost-of-inaction → quantified payoff; end when you can state the business case in one sentence the economic buyer would sign. Deal inspection before forecast: which buyer-step (evidence not label), EB met, champion proven, paper process mapped, compelling event dated, MAP live, single-threaded?, next step dated — end at a forecast *category*, not a feeling. Multithread to cover an unowned decision role, not to collect contacts. Close against indecision (JOLT): judge → offer a recommendation (don't expand optionality) → limit exploration → take risk off the table. Negotiate only after qualified, never to rescue a stall: open from a defended anchor, *trade never donate* (every concession conditional, pulls something back), know the walk-away. CRM hygiene: automation captures the record, a human owns interpretation; flag the signals (missing EB/champion, single-thread, stale open deal, no MAP on a Commit).

**Where senior diverges from junior:** No named economic-buyer-level authority *and* proven champion → not qualified, however warm one contact is. A clean "no" beats a slow "maybe" (the highest-leverage senior reflex — disqualify fast to return capacity). Multithreading is risk reduction, not relationship-collecting. The modern close is de-risking, not persuading — once intent exists the buyer avoids failure, so re-pitching "status quo is bad" addresses the wrong fear. Stall is the default outcome and its cause is internal buyer conflict, not the pitch (most lost complex deals are lost to "no decision"). Trade, don't cave — price is rarely the real blocker. Separate the deal verdict from hope — forecast what you'd stake money on. Personalisation bar: a company name is not personalisation; no specific true relevant hook → don't send. Match rigour to deal economics — full MEDDPICC on a transactional sale is friction that loses to a faster competitor; BANT-only on a seven-figure committee deal is negligence.

**Failure modes:** happy ears · single-threading · pipeline as a graveyard (dead deals left open to flatter the number) · sandbagging or padding the forecast · feature-dumping before diagnosing · mistaking a coach for a champion · skipping the paper process (the #1 silent enterprise slip) · no compelling event · caving on price to rescue a fear-of-failure stall · CRM as surveillance theatre (reps game fields they see no value in) · selling only new logos · chasing reactive inbound as a strategy (arriving late to a drawn shortlist).

## Forecasting & foresight

**Lenses:** match the mode to horizon × decidability — short horizon + data + resolvable → quantitative forecast (a probability/distribution); long horizon + deep uncertainty + unresolvable → strategic foresight (divergent scenarios for *readiness*, not prediction). The mode error (forecasting the unforecastable, or scenario-planning a question that wanted a number) is the most expensive mistake in the craft. Outside view before inside view — anchor to the reference class, *then* adjust cautiously; but check the class still holds (a structural break voids a base rate, and a dead reference class is worse than none). Three scenario schools, picked deliberately: Intuitive Logics (rich qualitative narratives — the strategy default), Probabilistic Modified Trends (assign probabilities, Monte-Carlo), La Prospective (structural cross-impact). Senior foresight lenses: Three Horizons, backcasting (goal known, path unknown), Causal Layered Analysis (litany → system → worldview → myth). Reflexivity (where a forecast feeds the system it forecasts, equilibrium models mislead) and black swans (consequential events are often fat-tailed unforecastables) — the senior response is antifragility/robustness, not better point-prediction. Leading vs lagging indicators; weak signals precede trends precede events.

**Method:** Quantitative forecast — triage (is it in the Goldilocks zone?) → Fermi-ize into sub-questions → outside view / base rate per part → adjust inside-view cautiously → state as a probability or interval *with a horizon and explicit assumptions* → schedule re-updates → **score against outcome** → recalibrate. Decompose then aggregate (independent estimates combined near-always lower error). Seasonal-naïve is the mandatory floor; ETS/ARIMA the next rung — report any model's delta over the floor or you're reporting nothing. Pick the score to match the loss (log punishes confident-and-wrong almost unboundedly; Brier is bounded; CRPS scores a full distribution — the score *is* a statement of what error you most fear). Backtest honestly — walk-forward / rolling-origin, strictly out-of-sample, no leakage. Reconcile the hierarchy (top-down vs bottom-up to one coherent set). Foresight cycle: frame the focal question + horizon → horizon-scan for signals → cluster into drivers → rank by impact × uncertainty → 3–4 divergent, coherent, *named* scenarios → wind-tunnel current strategy against each → extract early-warning indicators + trigger points.

**Where senior diverges from junior:** Maximise sharpness *subject to* calibration, in that order — a sharp miscalibrated forecaster is worse than a vague calibrated one. Intervals must fan out with horizon (fixed-width bands across horizons mean the uncertainty was asserted, not propagated). Granularity earns accuracy to a point — update often on real evidence, resist the lurch on a vivid headline; distinguish an evidence update from a mood update. Resolvability is a precondition — if it can't be scored against an unambiguous future fact, reframe it or route it to foresight. Forecast the *decision*, not the world. Name the trigger that flips you — a forecast without a pre-stated "what would change my mind" is a belief. Don't extrapolate through the inflection. Separate the forecast from the recommendation — "likely X" is the analysis's, "therefore do Y" is the decision-owner's.

**Failure modes:** false precision · narrative masquerading as probability (the conjunction fallacy — a vivid detailed scenario feels more probable while being less) · inside-view anchoring · dead reference class · mode mismatch · scenario axes that aren't uncertain or are two faces of one driver · predict-the-one-future · no closed loop (forecasts never scored, scenarios never wind-tunnelled — the most common organisational failure) · overfitting/leakage · recency & availability · hindsight laundering · aggregating correlated estimates.

## Cross-cutting: where the machine stops

- **Deterministic vs probabilistic split is the governing principle.** Arithmetic, postings, reconciliations, the final calculation run on rules (auditable); the model does analysis, narrative, anomaly-spotting, drafting — "the model did the math" is itself a control failure.
- **A human owns the regulated output.** Anything claim-bearing, money-moving, forecast-moving, or legally-binding runs *analysis → verify → human sign-off → act*, never *verify → act* — this is the sign-off duty applied to advisory work, and it holds even when nothing leaves the machine, because an acted-on number or legal position is irreversible in effect. The analysis states the number and the risk; the owner — or where the matter is regulated, a qualified professional — accepts it. Never smuggle the risk decision inside the analysis: the verdict and the risk-appetite call are separate artifacts, and the authority owns the accept.
- **Generated output is a draft to verify, never the answer.** Fabricated citations, hallucinated totals, and tail-overconfidence are known failure classes of the tool doing this work — verify the number against the primary source, recalibrate the probability deliberately, check the clause against the instrument.
- **Auditability is non-negotiable.** An output whose reasoning and tool-calls can't be reconstructed can't enter the books, the file, or the forecast of record.
- **Human judgment gets scarcer and more valuable as automation scales** — the variant perception, the named sacrifice, the real signal, the second-order effect, the regulated boundary. That is exactly where these disciplines live, and exactly what the machine is structurally blind to.

## Source-map (pointers, never copies — pull the live fact at the named trigger)

- **Strategy canon** — Rumelt (*Good Strategy/Bad Strategy*); Lafley & Martin (*Playing to Win*) + Martin's current writing on WTP/HTW; Helmer (*7 Powers*); Porter; Wardley; Christensen (JTBD). *Books durable; re-check the authors' current articles for refinements.* The institutional strategy press (McKinsey, BCG, HBR, Stratechery) for the *current* read on market structure and what's commoditising — the volatile layer.
- **Scenario & uncertainty methodology** — Courtney/Kirkland/Viguerie "Strategy Under Uncertainty" (four levels); Schwartz/GBN lineage (2×2 scenarios); Trigeorgis & McKinsey on real options.
- **Accounting standards** — IFRS (`ifrs.org`) / US GAAP (FASB `asc.fasb.org`) for recognition, measurement, presentation, current editions/effective dates. *Re-check on a new/amended standard.*
- **Tax & companies authority** (jurisdiction-specific — the national tax authority's rates pages; the company registrar) — current rates, thresholds, allowances, filing deadlines, digital-filing mandates. *Re-check at every fiscal event and before quoting any rate — point-in-time, to verify not infer.*
- **Controls / modelling / valuation** — COSO (`coso.org`); FAST Standard + ICAEW modelling guidance; a valuation authority (CFA curriculum, IVSC `ivsc.org`, Damodaran at `pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar`) for cost-of-capital, equity-risk-premium, current sector multiples/betas. *Re-check before sourcing a live discount-rate input or benchmark multiple.* The auditing/assurance standard setter (PCAOB/IAASB/national) for where a regulated opinion is needed.
- **Legal/regulatory primary sources** — the jurisdiction's primary legislation register (`legislation.gov.uk`, `eur-lex.europa.eu`, national equivalents); the financial-conduct regulator's handbook + policy statements (e.g. FCA); the data-protection authority (e.g. ICO, EDPB); the consumer-protection / competition authority (e.g. CMA, FTC) + the live penalty ceiling; the AI-regulation instrument + supervisory guidance (e.g. EU AI Act); the financial-crime statute + AML supervisor guidance (POCA/MLR + FATF); the recognised risk/governance bodies (COSO, ISO, IIA, NIST); the professional-conduct/ethics rules for the escalation and unauthorised-practice lines. *Re-check at project start for the governing jurisdiction and on any amendment signal — the consolidated text, not a summary, is the final word.*
- **Sales** — the connected CRM's own docs + live data (stage definitions, forecast categories, discount thresholds, actual conversion/velocity); the methodology primary sources (MEDDICC, SPIN, Challenger/JOLT, Gap Selling); buyer-behaviour research (Gartner B2B Buying, Forrester, Bain) for current committee size / stall rate / multithreading lift — *the most-cited-stale figures in sales*; email-deliverability standards (mailbox-provider bulk-sender rules, SPF/DKIM/DMARC RFCs) — a hard moving technical floor; the applicable outbound/marketing & data-protection law (GDPR/PECR, CAN-SPAM, CASL).
- **Forecasting** — Good Judgment / Tetlock (`goodjudgment.com`, *Superforecasting*) for the commandments and human-vs-AI track records; Metaculus / prediction markets for live base rates and the log-score convention; ForecastBench / `forecastingresearch.org` for LLM-vs-human benchmarks *(re-check before citing any AI-forecasting accuracy claim — the crossover date moves)*; Hyndman *FPP* (`otexts.com/fpp3`) for ETS/ARIMA, evaluation, hierarchical reconciliation; Gneiting & Raftery (2007) for proper scoring rules; the M-competitions (Makridakis) for the simple-vs-complex evidence; foresight bodies (Govt Office for Science Futures Toolkit, APF, IFTF) for horizon-scanning method. TS foundation-model docs (Nixtla, model cards) — *volatile; never freeze a model name as SOTA.*
- **The vault's own record** — prior DECISIONS, scored predictions, north-star, instance-specific rates/jurisdiction facts in `Vault/memory/` and the project files. *Re-check at task start so a settled call isn't relitigated and a logged forecast can be scored.*
